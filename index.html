Micro Mobility Data Transformation and Visualization

This section presents two of my class projects for my university course of Transport innovation for a sustainable, inclusive and smart mobility, by Prof. Cristina Pronello. We were to explore and visualize micro mobility patterns of two cities of Austin and Chicago. The datasets were near-big-data, with sizes of 15gbs for Austin and 20gbs for Chicago. The final report of the class for this exercise can be found at Google Doc. For Austin, I did the exploratory data analysis and feature engineering within Google Colab environment , proceeded with QGIS Map Visualizations. For Chicago, I followed a similar procedure, undertaking the data cleaning and feature engineering within Google Colab and Map Visualizations in QGIS. I also created an Interactive Visualiztion, enabled by FlowmapBlue, breaking down the origin-destination flow into the demographic dimension of Gender as well as temporal dimension of of weekday/weekend.

I made Two Animations of the two cities of Texas and Chicago for a better understanding of the cross sections of the origin-destination micro mobility flow. These two intensive exercises provided me with the chance to enhance my skills of acquiring/ingesting data, transforming data, and visualizing and reporting. They also built upon my critical thinking to understand the underlying reasons for certain characteristics of unknown populations.

TelegramBot-Enabled IoT System Design and Implementation

As part of the IoT and Cloud for Sustainable Communities course, instructed by Lorenzo Bottaccioli, I developed expertise in Object-Oriented Programming with Python and key machine-to-machine (M2M) communication paradigms. The course covered both request-response communication using CherryPy and the publish-subscribe model using paho-mqtt, reinforced through lab sessions and practical exercises.

For the final project, we built an Insulin Monitoring and Actuation System to store near-real-time glucose measurements in InfluxDB using the publish-subscribe paradigm. Users could retrieve past blood sugar statistics and manage insulin injections via a Telegram Bot, enhancing remote monitoring and control.

I was responsible for developing the sensor and actuator modules, enabling measurement publishing and remote actuation through POST requests. Additionally, I led the team and supported the implementation of the subscriber service, InfluxDB integration, and a catalogue service that registered clients and services. We designed the system following microservice architecture principles to ensure scalability and flexibility.

You can explore the project's Documentation and GitHub Repository.
